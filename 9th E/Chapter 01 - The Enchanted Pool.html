ಅಧ್ಯಾಯ 1: ದಿ ಎನ್‌ಚ್ಯಾಂಟೆಡ್ ಪೂಲ್ (The Enchanted Pool)

ಅಧ್ಯಾಯದ ಅವಲೋಕನ (Chapter Overview)

English Overview: "The Enchanted Pool" is an excerpt from C. Rajagopalachari's version of the Mahabharata. It narrates the story of the Pandavas during their exile. When the brothers become thirsty, they go in search of water but fall unconscious after drinking from a mysterious pool guarded by a Yaksha. Yudhistira, the eldest, eventually finds them, answers the Yaksha's profound questions with wisdom, and succeeds in reviving his brothers. The story highlights the importance of dharma, patience, and intelligence.

Kannada Translation: "ದಿ ಎನ್‌ಚ್ಯಾಂಟೆಡ್ ಪೂಲ್" ಸಿ. ರಾಜಗೋಪಾಲಾಚಾರಿ ಅವರ ಮಹಾಭಾರತದ ಒಂದು ಭಾಗವಾಗಿದೆ. ಇದು ಪಾಂಡವರು ಅಜ್ಞಾತವಾಸದಲ್ಲಿದ್ದಾಗ ನಡೆದ ಘಟನೆಯನ್ನು ವಿವರಿಸುತ್ತದೆ. ಪಾಂಡವರು ಬಾಯಾರಿಕೆಯಿಂದ ನೀರನ್ನು ಹುಡುಕುತ್ತಾ ಹೋಗಿ, ಯಕ್ಷನೊಬ್ಬನ ಕಾವಲಿನಲ್ಲಿರುವ ನಿಗೂಢ ಕೊಳದ ನೀರನ್ನು ಕುಡಿದು ಪ್ರಜ್ಞೆ ತಪ್ಪುತ್ತಾರೆ. ಕೊನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಧರ್ಮರಾಜ (ಯುಧಿಷ್ಠಿರ) ಅಲ್ಲಿಗೆ ಬಂದು, ಯಕ್ಷನ ಪ್ರಶ್ನೆಗಳಿಗೆ ತನ್ನ ಜ್ಞಾನದಿಂದ ಉತ್ತರಿಸಿ, ತನ್ನ ಸಹೋದರರನ್ನು ಮರಳಿ ಪಡೆಯುತ್ತಾನೆ. ಈ ಕಥೆಯು ಧರ್ಮ, ತಾಳ್ಮೆ ಮತ್ತು ಬುದ್ಧಿವಂತಿಕೆಯ ಮಹತ್ವವನ್ನು ಸಾರುತ್ತದೆ.

C3. Identify the Speaker (ಪುಟ 10)

  1. "Please ask your questions." - Yudhistira (Quality: Humility)

  3. "May the cloud-complexioned... Nakula arise." - Yudhistira (Quality: Selflessness)

C4. Short Paragraph Answers (ಪುಟ 10)

1. Nakula's conclusion: Nakula saw water plants and cranes at a distance. Since these creatures and plants thrive near water, he logically concluded that there must be a pool or river nearby.

2. Arjuna's desire: Arjuna was a great warrior. He felt challenged by the invisible voice and wanted to protect his brothers and himself by destroying the source of the threat.

3. Yudhistira's decision: Yudhistira was worried because none of his brothers returned. He decided to go himself to find out what had happened to them.

4. Happiness and good conduct: Yes, I agree. Good conduct leads to peace of mind and respect from others, which are the foundations of true happiness. For example, being honest avoids guilt and stress.

5. Choosing Nakula: Yudhistira was fair. He knew that he (a son of Kunti) was alive. To ensure justice for his stepmother Madri, he chose her son Nakula to be revived.

Q1: Describe the character of Yudhistira as revealed in the story. (Long Answer)

Yudhistira is portrayed as the embodiment of wisdom, patience, and righteousness. Unlike his brothers, who acted impulsively due to thirst and anger, Yudhistira remained calm and composed. He showed great respect for the invisible voice and chose to answer the questions rather than fight. His decision to revive Nakula instead of Bhima or Arjuna demonstrates his deep sense of justice and impartiality. He valued his stepmother's feelings as much as his own mother's. His ability to answer profound philosophical questions shows his intellectual depth. Ultimately, his adherence to dharma (righteousness) saved his brothers and earned him the blessings of Yama.

Q2: Why did the Yaksha test the Pandavas? Explain the significance of the test. (Long Answer)

The Yaksha, who was actually Yama (the Lord of Death), tested the Pandavas to see if they possessed the qualities required of a true leader and a righteous person. The test was significant because it taught the brothers that physical strength and prowess in arms are not enough to overcome life's challenges. One needs wisdom, patience, and control over one's senses. By failing to heed the warning, the brothers showed their lack of restraint. Yudhistira, by passing the test, proved that he had mastered his ego and anger. The test served as a moral lesson that dharma is the only thing that accompanies a soul in its journey after death.

Q3: What are the moral lessons we learn from "The Enchanted Pool"? (Long Answer)

The story teaches us several vital moral lessons. Firstly, it emphasizes the importance of patience and listening to warnings. Impulsiveness, as shown by the brothers, leads to disaster. Secondly, it teaches that true strength lies in character and good conduct, not just in physical power. Thirdly, the story highlights the value of impartiality and fairness in relationships. Yudhistira's choice of Nakula teaches us to treat everyone with equal respect. Finally, it teaches us that anger and pride are obstacles to happiness and success. By abandoning these negative traits and following the path of dharma, one can overcome even the most difficult situations in life.
