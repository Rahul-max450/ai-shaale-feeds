Chapter Overview: Justice by Praveen Kumar

English Overview

The poem "Justice" by Praveen Kumar is a profound reflection on the moral decay of justice in contemporary society. The poet argues that justice is an inherent right and should never be treated as a commodity to be bought or sold. He laments how justice is often shackled by greed, bribery, and the influence of popular opinion. Through powerful imagery and metaphors, the poet compares justice to gold buried in mud, suggesting that while it may be obscured by corruption, its intrinsic value remains. The poem serves as a wake-up call, asserting that justice without truth and a commitment to the right cause is essentially dead.

Section 2: Poem Memory Support

1. Poem Summary

The poem "Justice" explores the sanctity of justice. The poet emphasizes that justice is not a trade; it is an inherent human right. He observes that in the modern world, justice is often compromised by greed and corruption. He uses metaphors like "gold strains bound in mud" to show that justice is pure but currently trapped in a corrupt system. The poem concludes that if justice loses its heart for truth and its courage to stand for the right cause, it ceases to exist.

2. Central Idea

The central idea is the preservation of justice as a moral value. It highlights the conflict between justice as an inherent right and justice as a commodity sold for profit.

3. Theme

The themes include the corruption of legal systems, the intrinsic value of truth, the struggle for survival in a materialistic world, and the moral responsibility of society to uphold justice.

4. Poet Message

Praveen Kumar conveys that justice must be independent of greed and bribery. He urges readers to recognize that justice is a living value that dies when it is manipulated for personal gain.

5. Easy Memorization Notes

  • Stanza 1: Justice is not trade; it is an inherent right.
  • Stanza 2: Justice is like gold in mud; it is currently exploited.
  • Stanza 3: Justice is cool like ice and firm like rock; it is hard to influence.
  • Stanza 4: Justice is trapped by greed and bribery; it is lost in darkness.
  • Stanza 5: Justice without truth and courage is dead.

6. 5 Mark Answer

The poet Praveen Kumar presents justice as an inherent right that should not be commodified. He argues that when justice is exchanged for money, it becomes a trade, which is a "distressing gain through loss." He compares justice to gold hidden in mud, implying that although it is currently obscured by the corruption of the modern world, its true value remains. The poet expresses deep concern that justice is being shackled by greed and bribery, losing its way in the "thick jungle of lightless night." He emphasizes that for justice to remain alive, it must possess a heart for truth and the courage to fight for the right cause. Without these qualities, justice is merely a dead concept.

7. 10 Mark Answer

In the poem "Justice," Praveen Kumar provides a scathing critique of how justice is treated in contemporary society. He begins by asserting that justice is an inherent right, not a commodity to be bought or sold. By using the phrase "A distressing gain through loss," he highlights that any profit made by compromising justice is a moral loss for humanity. The poet employs vivid imagery, comparing justice to "gold strains bound in mud," suggesting that while justice is pure and valuable, it is currently trapped under the weight of societal corruption. He further describes justice as being "cool like ice" and "still like rock," indicating its potential for steadfastness, yet he laments that it is currently "shackled to greed and bribe." The poet uses metaphors like a "rat caught in the sack of death" and a "deer caught in lion’s lair" to illustrate the helplessness of justice in the face of powerful, corrupt forces. He argues that justice cannot reach its goal by itself when it is manipulated by popular moods or dark influences. Ultimately, the poet concludes that justice is not merely a legal process but a moral one. If it lacks a heart for truth and the "dash" (courage) to stand for the right cause, it is effectively dead. The poem serves as a powerful reminder that the survival of a just society depends on our ability to protect justice from being exploited for personal or material gain.

Answer: The poet's message that justice must have a "heart for truth" is the core of the poem. He argues that legal procedures, rules, and regulations are meaningless if they are not rooted in a genuine commitment to truth. A "heart for truth" implies empathy, integrity, and a moral compass that guides the decision-making process. Without this, justice becomes a mechanical, cold, and dead process that serves only those who can afford it. The poet suggests that justice is not just about following the letter of the law, but about understanding the spirit of fairness. When justice loses its heart, it becomes a "sack of death" or a "lion's lair," where the innocent are trapped and the guilty escape. Thus, the poet calls for a revival of moral conscience in the pursuit of justice, ensuring that it remains a living, breathing force for good.
