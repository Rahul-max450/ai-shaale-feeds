UNIT - 7: The Will of Sacrifice

SECTION 1 : CHAPTER OVERVIEW

English Overview

The chapter "The Will of Sacrifice" is a biographical account of the legendary Indian revolutionary, Bhagat Singh. It traces his life from his birth in Khatkar Kalan to his martyrdom. The text highlights his early exposure to the freedom struggle through his family, his association with prominent revolutionaries, and his ideological shift towards socialism. The chapter includes two poignant letters written by Bhagat Singh from the Lahore Central Jail, which reveal his unwavering commitment to the cause of Indian independence, his intellectual depth, and his readiness to embrace the gallows for the sake of his nation. It emphasizes that true value lies in what one gives to society rather than what one receives.

Question 1: Describe the ideological beliefs of Bhagat Singh as a thinker.
Answer: Bhagat Singh was not just a revolutionary but a profound thinker deeply influenced by socialist traditions. He believed in the collective ownership of the means of production, such as land, labour, and capital. He clarified that 'revolution' did not mean mere violence, but rather the 'spirit of freedom' and a 'longing for a change for the better.' His writings from prison reveal a man who was intellectually committed to creating a just and equitable society, proving that his actions were guided by a clear vision for India's future beyond just political independence.

Question 2: Why did Bhagat Singh consider his death a 'final test'?
Answer: Bhagat Singh viewed his impending execution as a 'final test' because he wanted to demonstrate to the world that revolutionaries could face death with a smile. He believed that if he escaped the gallows, his weaknesses might be exposed, tarnishing the symbol of revolution he had become. By mounting the gallows boldly, he aimed to inspire Indian mothers to raise children who would follow in his footsteps, thereby ensuring that the tide of revolution would become unstoppable for the British imperialists.

Question 3: How did the letters written by Bhagat Singh from jail reflect his character?
Answer: The letters written by Bhagat Singh from the Lahore Central Jail reflect his immense courage, intellectual maturity, and selfless patriotism. They show that he was not afraid of death; rather, he embraced it as a necessary sacrifice for his ideals. His concern for his comrades and his desire to see the revolution continue even after his death highlight his leadership qualities. These letters serve as a testament to his character, proving that he was a man who valued the nation's freedom above his own life.
