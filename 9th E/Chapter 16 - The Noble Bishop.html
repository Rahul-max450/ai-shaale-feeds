UNIT - 6: The Noble Bishop

SECTION 1: CHAPTER OVERVIEW

English Overview

The play "The Noble Bishop," adapted from Victor Hugo's classic, tells the story of Jean Valjean, a man who has spent nineteen years in prison for the simple crime of stealing bread to feed his starving family. Upon his release, he is treated as an outcast by society. He arrives at the home of a kind Bishop, who treats him with compassion and dignity, calling him 'friend' and offering him food and shelter. Despite this kindness, Valjean steals the Bishop's silverware in the middle of the night. When he is caught by the police, the Bishop saves him by claiming he gave the silver as a gift and even offers him his silver candlesticks. This act of profound mercy transforms Valjean, leading him to abandon his life of bitterness and become an honest, compassionate man.
