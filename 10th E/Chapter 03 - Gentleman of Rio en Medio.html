Chapter 03: Gentleman of Rio en Medio

Overview

The story "Gentleman of Rio en Medio" by Juan A.A. Sedillo is a poignant narrative about a humble, elderly man named Don Anselmo. He owns a piece of land in Rio en Medio that he decides to sell to a group of Americans. Despite the Americans offering him double the agreed price after discovering the land is larger than expected, Don Anselmo refuses to take more money, sticking strictly to his original agreement. Later, the Americans face trouble with the village children playing in their orchard. It is revealed that Don Anselmo had planted a tree for every child born in the village, and he considers those trees to belong to the children, not to the land he sold. The story highlights themes of integrity, cultural values, and the deep connection between people, nature, and their heritage.

Additional Exam-Oriented Questions

1. Describe the character of Don Anselmo as a man of principles. (5 Marks)

Answer: Don Anselmo is a man of high principles. He values his word more than money. When the Americans offer him double the amount for his land, he feels insulted because he believes that his word is his bond. He had agreed to a price, and he sticks to it. Furthermore, his love for the village children and his commitment to the trees he planted for them show his selfless nature. He is not motivated by greed but by a sense of duty to his community and his ancestors. His dignity, patience, and unwavering honesty make him a truly noble character.

2. Why did the Americans find it difficult to live in the house they bought? (5 Marks)

Answer: The Americans found it difficult because the village children were constantly overrunning their property. The children played under the trees, built fences, and plucked blossoms. When the Americans tried to stop them, the children would laugh and speak in Spanish. The Americans felt their privacy was being invaded. They had bought the land expecting complete possession, but the children's presence made them feel like they were not the true owners. This conflict arose because Don Anselmo had never sold the trees, and the children believed the trees belonged to them.

3. Explain the significance of the title "Gentleman of Rio en Medio". (5 Marks)

Answer: The title is highly appropriate because Don Anselmo embodies the true qualities of a gentleman. He is polite, respectful, and dignified in his interactions. He bows to everyone, removes his hat and gloves with care, and speaks with grace. Despite his poverty and his ragged clothes, he carries himself with an air of nobility. His integrity in business dealings and his deep love for his community distinguish him as a man of honor. He is a "gentleman" not because of his wealth, but because of his character, his respect for others, and his commitment to his values.

4. How did the storyteller resolve the issue of the trees? (5 Marks)

Answer: After realizing that Don Anselmo would not sell the trees because they belonged to the children, the storyteller had to find another way. He realized that legally the Americans owned the trees, but morally they belonged to the descendants of Don Anselmo. The storyteller spent the entire winter tracking down every single descendant of Don Anselmo in the valley of Rio en Medio. He bought the trees from each of them individually. This was a long and tedious process, but it was the only way to ensure the Americans could have full possession of the property while respecting the old man's wishes.

5. Discuss the cultural clash between the Americans and the villagers. (5 Marks)

Answer: The cultural clash is evident in how the two groups view property and nature. The Americans view land as a commodity that can be bought, sold, and fully possessed. They believe that buying the land gives them absolute rights over everything on it, including the trees. On the other hand, the villagers, led by Don Anselmo, view the land and the trees as a shared heritage. For them, the trees are living beings connected to the children of the village. This difference in perspective leads to the conflict where the Americans feel their rights are being violated, while the villagers feel they are protecting their community's soul.
