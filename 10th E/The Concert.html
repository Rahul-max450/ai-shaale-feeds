English Overview

The chapter "The Concert" by Shantha Rameshwar Rao is a poignant story about a fifteen-year-old boy named Anant, who is suffering from cancer. Despite his illness, his passion for music, specifically the sitar, remains undiminished. His sister, Smita, discovers that the legendary Pandit Ravi Shankar is performing in Bombay. Anant, being too weak to attend, expresses his deep desire to hear the maestro. Smita, driven by love and a daring spirit, approaches Pandit Ravi Shankar after his concert and requests him to perform for her dying brother. Moved by her plea, the maestro and Ustad Allah Rakha visit Anant's home the next morning and perform for him, bringing him immense happiness in his final moments.

Music is often called the universal language, and the poem "Music" serves as a testament to this truth. The poet explores how music transcends mere sound to become a vital component of human life. Firstly, it is identified as a source of love and life, suggesting that our existence is rhythmic and harmonious. Secondly, the poem highlights the narrative quality of music—it tells stories and triggers memories, acting as a vessel for our past experiences. Thirdly, the therapeutic aspect is emphasized; music has the unique ability to soothe the soul and alleviate physical or emotional pain. When one listens to music, they are transported to "another world," indicating its power to provide escapism and tranquility. The poem inspires the reader to actively engage with music, asking, "Do you hear it?" and inviting them to "dance." This signifies that music is not meant to be observed passively but to be lived and felt. Ultimately, the poem conveys that music is the heartbeat of our lives, providing inspiration, healing, and a sense of belonging in a complex world.
