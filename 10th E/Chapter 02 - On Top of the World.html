English Overview

The chapter "On Top of the World" is an autobiographical account by Dicky Dolma, who became the youngest woman to scale Mount Everest in 1993 at the age of 19. Born in Palchan, Himachal Pradesh, Dolma faced significant personal tragedies, including the loss of her mother and brother at a young age. Despite these hardships, her fascination with the Himalayas and her background in skiing fueled her passion for mountaineering. The chapter details her rigorous training at the Manali mountaineering institute, her financial struggles, and her unwavering determination. It highlights that success is a product of hard work, sincerity, and a refusal to shirk responsibilities.
