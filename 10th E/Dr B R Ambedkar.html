Question 4: Write a short note on Dr. Ambedkar’s idea/perception of the three pillars of State.

Question Meaning in Kannada: ರಾಜ್ಯದ ಮೂರು ಸ್ತಂಭಗಳ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಡಾ. ಅಂಬೇಡ್ಕರ್ ಅವರ ಕಲ್ಪನೆ/ಗ್ರಹಿಕೆಯ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಸಣ್ಣ ಟಿಪ್ಪಣಿ ಬರೆಯಿರಿ.

English Answer: (5 Marks) Dr. Ambedkar had a clear perception of the mutuality of the three pillars of the State: the legislature, the executive, and the judiciary. He believed that the jurisdiction of each organ should be clear and untrammelled. He emphasized that the Constitution is a fundamental document that defines the powers of these three organs. Crucially, he argued that the purpose of the Constitution is not just to create these organs but to limit their authority. SECTION 4: EXTRA EXAM QUESTIONS

Question 1: Discuss the role of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar in the making of the Indian Constitution.
Answer: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Constituent Assembly. He played a pivotal role in shaping the Constitution. He meticulously studied the constitutions of various nations and adapted them to the unique needs of India. He raised the edifice of Fundamental Rights, ensuring the protection of individual liberties. He possessed the rare ability to explain complex legal concepts in simple language. His patience, tact, and frankness during the assembly debates were instrumental in building consensus. He is rightly remembered as the 'pilot' of the Indian Constitution, as he guided the entire process with vision and dedication.

Question 2: Why did Dr. Ambedkar emphasize that the Constitution must limit the authority of the State organs?
Answer: Dr. Ambedkar believed that the primary purpose of a Constitution is not merely to create the organs of the State—the legislature, the executive, and the judiciary—but to define and limit their authority. He argued that if these organs were given absolute power without any constitutional limitations, it would inevitably lead to tyranny and oppression. By defining the powers of these organs in relation to the citizens, the Constitution acts as a safeguard against the abuse of power. This ensures that the government remains accountable and that the rights and dignity of the citizens are protected within a democratic framework.

Question 3: How did the "Divide and Rule" policy of the British affect the caste system in India?
Answer: The British rulers, to strengthen their hegemony over India, actively employed the "Divide and Rule" policy. They exaggerated existing caste distinctions and created further divisions among the people to prevent national unity. By magnifying these differences, they made it easier to control the population and maintain their colonial rule. This policy entrenched the caste system even deeper into the social fabric of India. It was only through the concerted efforts of leaders like Mahatma Gandhi and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, who worked to repudiate these divisions and proclaim the oneness of the Hindu community, that the nation began to move towards social integration.
