Class: 10th English • CHAPTER 08 - THE SONG OF INDIA

SECTION 1: CHAPTER OVERVIEW

English Overview

The poem "The Song of India" by V.K. Gokak is a patriotic dialogue between the poet and Mother India. The poet wishes to sing about the glorious past, natural beauty, and modern technological achievements of India. However, Mother India insists that he must also acknowledge the harsh realities of poverty, ignorance, and social conflict. The poem concludes with a visionary image of India's future, where the Motherland overcomes all struggles to write a new, bright destiny.

Kannada Translation (ಕನ್ನಡ ಅನುವಾದ)

ವಿ.ಕೆ. ಗೋಕಾಕ್ ಅವರ "ದಿ ಸಾಂಗ್ ಆಫ್ ಇಂಡಿಯಾ" ಕವಿತೆಯು ಕವಿ ಮತ್ತು ಭಾರತ ಮಾತೆಯ ನಡುವಿನ ದೇಶಭಕ್ತಿಯ ಸಂಭಾಷಣೆಯಾಗಿದೆ. ಕವಿಯು ಭಾರತದ ಭವ್ಯವಾದ ಇತಿಹಾಸ, ನೈಸರ್ಗಿಕ ಸೌಂದರ್ಯ ಮತ್ತು ಆಧುನಿಕ ತಾಂತ್ರಿಕ ಸಾಧನೆಗಳ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಹಾಡಲು ಬಯಸುತ್ತಾನೆ. ಆದರೆ, ಭಾರತ ಮಾತೆ ಕವಿಗೆ ಬಡತನ, ಅಜ್ಞಾನ ಮತ್ತು ಸಾಮಾಜಿಕ ಸಂಘರ್ಷಗಳಂತಹ ಕಠಿಣ ವಾಸ್ತವಗಳನ್ನು ಕೂಡ ಗುರುತಿಸಬೇಕೆಂದು ಒತ್ತಾಯಿಸುತ್ತಾಳೆ. ಕವಿತೆಯು ಭಾರತದ ಭವಿಷ್ಯದ ದಾರ್ಶನಿಕ ಚಿತ್ರಣದೊಂದಿಗೆ ಕೊನೆಗೊಳ್ಳುತ್ತದೆ, ಅಲ್ಲಿ ಭಾರತ ಮಾತೆ ಎಲ್ಲಾ ಹೋರಾಟಗಳನ್ನು ಮೀರಿ ಹೊಸ ಮತ್ತು ಉಜ್ವಲ ಭವಿಷ್ಯವನ್ನು ಬರೆಯುತ್ತಾಳೆ.

SECTION 2: POEM MEMORY SUPPORT

1. Poem Summary

The poem is a dialogue between the poet and Mother India. The poet initially wants to praise India's natural beauty (Himalayas, seas) and historical grandeur (temples, martyrs). Mother India corrects him, asking him to sing about the suffering of the poor, the lepers, and the ignorant. When the poet mentions modern progress (dams, steel mills), she asks him to also sing about class wars and strikes. Finally, the poet asks for a song that is pure and eternal. Mother India then rises in a divine vision, writing the 'Book of the Morrow,' symbolizing a future where sorrow is cancelled and a new dawn begins.

2. Central Idea

The central idea is that true patriotism involves accepting both the glory and the struggles of one's country. One cannot ignore the social evils while celebrating the achievements; both must be acknowledged to build a better future.

3. Theme

The theme revolves around patriotism, the duality of reality (beauty vs. suffering), and the hope for a progressive, enlightened future for India.

4. Poet Message

The poet conveys that we should love our country not just for its beauty, but also work towards solving its problems. We must be honest about our nation's challenges to truly contribute to its growth.

5. Easy Memorization Notes

  • Stanza 1: Poet asks about nature; Mother points to poverty.
  • Stanza 2: Poet asks about history/martyrs; Mother points to ignorance/child labor.
  • Stanza 3: Poet asks about technology; Mother points to class wars.
  • Stanza 4: The vision of the future; the dawn of a new era.

6. 5 Mark Answer: The Vision of the Future

In the final stanza, the poet presents a powerful vision of India's future. Mother India rises, draped in the blue sky, with the milk-white oceans heaving around her. This imagery suggests a divine and majestic transformation. She sits and writes the 'Book of the Morrow,' which signifies that the future of India is being shaped by her own hands. The 'forehead opening like earth's destiny' and the 'Sun-God' appearing represent the arrival of knowledge, enlightenment, and the end of all sorrow. The 'clear dawn' symbolizes the end of the 'nightmare' of the past, and the 'sun-beam' acts as a saving hand, indicating that India is moving toward a bright, hopeful, and prosperous future.

7. 10 Mark Answer: Critical Analysis of the Poem

V.K. Gokak’s "The Song of India" is a profound exploration of patriotism. The poem is structured as a dialogue between the poet and Mother India. The poet represents the common citizen who is often blinded by the superficial beauty and historical pride of the nation. He wants to sing of the Himalayas, the seas, and the rock-cut temples. However, Mother India acts as the conscience of the nation. She forces the poet to look at the 'beggar,' the 'leper,' the 'wrinkled face of ignorance,' and the 'helpless child.' She reminds him that a country is not just its monuments, but its people. When the poet shifts to modern achievements like dams and steel mills, she again balances this by pointing out the 'class-war' and 'strikes.' The poet’s journey from being 'querulous' to seeking a 'stainless' song shows his growth in understanding. The final vision is the most significant part of the poem; it transforms the Mother from a suffering figure into a divine creator writing the 'Book of the Morrow.' This signifies that India’s destiny is not fixed; it is being written through the collective effort of its people. The poem serves as a reminder that true love for one's country requires both pride in its achievements and a commitment to addressing its social ills.

Question: What is the poet saying about India in the above lines? Discuss.

Question Meaning in Kannada: ಮೇಲಿನ ಸಾಲುಗಳಲ್ಲಿ ಕವಿ ಭಾರತದ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಏನು ಹೇಳುತ್ತಿದ್ದಾರೆ? ಚರ್ಚಿಸಿ.

English Answer: The poet expresses deep love for India, stating that it is better than Heaven or Arcadia. He emphasizes that this love should not be confined to oneself but should be shared with every brother nation in the world, promoting universal brotherhood.

Kannada Answer: ಕವಿಯು ಭಾರತದ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಅಪಾರ ಪ್ರೀತಿಯನ್ನು ವ್ಯಕ್ತಪಡಿಸುತ್ತಾ, ಅದು ಸ್ವರ್ಗ ಅಥವಾ ಆರ್ಕಾಡಿಯಾಕ್ಕಿಂತಲೂ ಶ್ರೇಷ್ಠವಾಗಿದೆ ಎಂದು ಹೇಳುತ್ತಾರೆ. ಈ ಪ್ರೀತಿಯು ಕೇವಲ ತನಗೆ ಮಾತ್ರ ಸೀಮಿತವಾಗಿರಬಾರದು, ಬದಲಾಗಿ ಪ್ರಪಂಚದ ಪ್ರತಿಯೊಂದು ಸಹೋದರ ರಾಷ್ಟ್ರದೊಂದಿಗೆ ಹಂಚಿಕೊಳ್ಳಬೇಕು ಎಂದು ಅವರು ಒತ್ತಿ ಹೇಳುತ್ತಾರೆ.

Question: 1. Identify the two speakers in the poem. What does the speaker want to sing about?

Question Meaning in Kannada: ಕವಿತೆಯಲ್ಲಿರುವ ಇಬ್ಬರು ಭಾಷಣಕಾರರನ್ನು ಗುರುತಿಸಿ. ಕವಿ ಯಾವುದರ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಹಾಡಲು ಬಯಸುತ್ತಾರೆ?

English Answer: The two speakers are the poet and Mother India. The poet wants to sing about the natural beauty of India (Himalayas, seas), its historical and cultural heritage (rock-cut temples, martyrs, seers), and its modern technological progress (dams, steel mills, Atomic Age).

Kannada Answer: ಕವಿತೆಯ ಇಬ್ಬರು ಭಾಷಣಕಾರರು ಕವಿ ಮತ್ತು ಭಾರತ ಮಾತೆ. ಕವಿಯು ಭಾರತದ ನೈಸರ್ಗಿಕ ಸೌಂದರ್ಯ (ಹಿಮಾಲಯ, ಸಮುದ್ರಗಳು), ಅದರ ಐತಿಹಾಸಿಕ ಮತ್ತು ಸಾಂಸ್ಕೃತಿಕ ಪರಂಪರೆ (ಶಿಲ್ಪಕಲೆ ದೇವಾಲಯಗಳು, ಹುತಾತ್ಮರು, ದಾರ್ಶನಿಕರು) ಮತ್ತು ಅದರ ಆಧುನಿಕ ತಾಂತ್ರಿಕ ಪ್ರಗತಿಯ (ಅಣೆಕಟ್ಟುಗಳು, ಉಕ್ಕಿನ ಕಾರ್ಖಾನೆಗಳು, ಪರಮಾಣು ಯುಗ) ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಹಾಡಲು ಬಯಸುತ್ತಾರೆ.

Question: 2. What are the epics? Why does the poet call the temples as ‘epics in stone’?

Question Meaning in Kannada: ಮಹಾಕಾವ್ಯಗಳು ಯಾವುವು? ಕವಿ ದೇವಾಲಯಗಳನ್ನು 'ಕಲ್ಲಿನ ಮಹಾಕಾವ್ಯಗಳು' ಎಂದು ಏಕೆ ಕರೆಯುತ್ತಾರೆ?

English Answer: Epics are long narrative poems recounting heroic deeds. The poet calls temples 'epics in stone' because they are grand, permanent structures that tell the stories of our culture, history, and spiritual devotion, just as epics tell stories through words.

Kannada Answer: ಮಹಾಕಾವ್ಯಗಳು ವೀರರ ಸಾಹಸಗಾಥೆಗಳನ್ನು ಹೇಳುವ ಸುದೀರ್ಘ ಕವಿತೆಗಳಾಗಿವೆ. ದೇವಾಲಯಗಳು ನಮ್ಮ ಸಂಸ್ಕೃತಿ, ಇತಿಹಾಸ ಮತ್ತು ಆಧ್ಯಾತ್ಮಿಕ ಭಕ್ತಿಯ ಕಥೆಗಳನ್ನು ಹೇಳುವ ಭವ್ಯವಾದ ಮತ್ತು ಶಾಶ್ವತ ರಚನೆಗಳಾಗಿರುವುದರಿಂದ ಕವಿ ಅವುಗಳನ್ನು 'ಕಲ್ಲಿನ ಮಹಾಕಾವ್ಯಗಳು' ಎಂದು ಕರೆಯುತ್ತಾರೆ.

Question: 5. Why is the poet ‘querulous’? What does he want to do?

Question Meaning in Kannada: ಕವಿ ಏಕೆ 'ದೂರು ನೀಡುವವರಂತೆ' (querulous) ಇದ್ದಾರೆ? ಅವರು ಏನು ಮಾಡಲು ಬಯಸುತ್ತಾರೆ?

English Answer: The poet is 'querulous' because he is frustrated that every time he tries to praise India, Mother India points out the negative realities. He wants to sing a song that is 'heart-whole' and 'unalloyed'—a song of pure, unblemished praise for his country.

Kannada Answer: ಕವಿಯು 'ದೂರು ನೀಡುವವರಂತೆ' ಇರುತ್ತಾರೆ ಏಕೆಂದರೆ ಅವರು ಭಾರತವನ್ನು ಹೊಗಳಲು ಪ್ರಯತ್ನಿಸಿದಾಗಲೆಲ್ಲಾ, ಭಾರತ ಮಾತೆ ನಕಾರಾತ್ಮಕ ವಾಸ್ತವಗಳನ್ನು ಎತ್ತಿ ತೋರಿಸುತ್ತಾಳೆ. ಅವರು 'ಪೂರ್ಣ ಹೃದಯದ' ಮತ್ತು 'ಕಲ್ಮಶವಿಲ್ಲದ' ಹಾಡನ್ನು ಹಾಡಲು ಬಯಸುತ್ತಾರೆ - ಅಂದರೆ ತಮ್ಮ ದೇಶಕ್ಕಾಗಿ ಶುದ್ಧವಾದ, ಕಳಂಕವಿಲ್ಲದ ಹೊಗಳಿಕೆಯ ಹಾಡು.

Question: 2. How does the poet describe the Mother's anger? Name the figure of speech used in stanza 2.
