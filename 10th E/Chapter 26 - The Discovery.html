SECTION 1: CHAPTER OVERVIEW

English Overview

The play "The Discovery" by Herman Ould depicts the final, tense moments of Christopher Columbus's voyage to the New World. It highlights the intense conflict between Columbus, a visionary leader, and his mutinous crew, who have lost faith and patience after months at sea. The play explores themes of leadership, faith, discipline, and the loneliness of a visionary. It culminates in the discovery of land, which validates Columbus's unwavering belief in his mission.

Question 1: Describe the character of Christopher Columbus as portrayed in the play "The Discovery".
