Chapter Overview: Babar Ali

English Overview:
This chapter profiles Babar Ali, a young boy from Murshidabad, West Bengal, who is recognized as the world's youngest headmaster. At the age of 16, while still a student himself, Babar Ali runs a school called "Anand Siksha Niketan" in his family's backyard. The chapter highlights his dedication to providing free education to underprivileged children who are otherwise forced into child labor. It explores his journey from starting a "school-school" game at age nine to managing a registered institution with 800 students and volunteer teachers. The narrative serves as an inspiring testament to how one individual's determination can spark significant social change.
